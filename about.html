About WorldPulse

WorldPulse is a live international news dashboard that brings real-time headlines, world clocks, and top global news outlets together in one clean, distraction-free interface. No noise, no clutter — just the pulse of the world.

Why we built this

Staying informed about global events shouldn't mean jumping between a dozen tabs and apps. WorldPulse aggregates the most important sources into a single dashboard so you can scan the world's headlines in seconds and dive deeper wherever you choose.

What you get

  • Live Feed: Real-time top stories fetched from the Hacker News API, auto-refreshing every 5 minutes.
  • World Clocks: Live time across New York, London, Berlin, Dubai, Beijing, Tokyo, and Sydney.
  • Top Outlets: One-tap access to 16 leading international news sources — Reuters, BBC, AP, Al Jazeera, Bloomberg, and more.
  • Smart Search: Search across Google News, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Reuters, and BBC without leaving the page.
  • Category Filters: Quickly drill into World, Tech, Business, Science, or Sports.

How it works

This is a fully static site hosted on GitHub Pages. The live feed is powered by the public Hacker News API and fetched directly in your browser — no server, no tracking, no data collection. Outlet links take you directly to the respective publishers.

Feedback

WorldPulse is actively evolving. If you'd like to suggest an outlet, report a broken link, or share an idea, please reach out via the contact page.
